Procedure

  1. Pour oil in a pot and begin heating it. You want it to be hot in about 3 minutes.
  2. Place all ingredients into a bowl or pot that can withstand high temperatures. However, save about 3 sesame seeds.
  3. Stir ingredients until they are consistent.
  4. Toss a single sesame seed into the heated oil.
    • If the seed burns, the oil is too hot. Turn off the fire to let it cool and test another seed later.
    • If the seed shows no signs of sizzling, the oil is too cold. Continue heating and test another seed later.
    • If the seed sizzles to an ever so slight shade of brown, the oil is at a good temperature. Proceed to step 5.
  5. Pour a small amount of your oil into the bowl and immediately stir the bubbles away.
  6. Pour a slightly larger amount of your oil into the bowl than before and immediately stir the bubbles away.
  7. Continue to pour more and more oil into the bowl with stirring until it has all been poured.
  8. The chili oil will be ready for consumption after it cools!

Ingredients
